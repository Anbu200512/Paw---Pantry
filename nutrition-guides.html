Got Questions?

Ask Our
Nutritionist

How much should I feed my dog each day?
Portion sizes depend on your dog's weight, age, activity level, and the specific food's calorie density. As a general rule, adult dogs need about 25-30 calories per pound of body weight per day. Always check the feeding guide on your food's packaging and consult your veterinarian for personalized recommendations.
Can I mix wet and dry food?
Absolutely! Combining wet and dry food can add variety, increase hydration, and make meals more appealing. Just be sure to adjust portions to avoid overfeeding — count the calories from both types. Many pet parents find a 75% dry / 25% wet mix works well.
What ingredients should I avoid?
Watch out for artificial preservatives (BHA, BHT, ethoxyquin), artificial colors, corn syrup, propylene glycol, and unspecified "meat by-products." Look for named protein sources (e.g., "chicken" not "poultry meal") and whole food ingredients you recognize. When in doubt, if you wouldn't eat it, your dog probably shouldn't either.
Is grain-free food better for all dogs?
Not necessarily. Grain-free diets are beneficial for dogs with grain allergies or sensitivities, but most dogs digest grains like rice and oats perfectly well. The key is the quality of ingredients, not whether grains are present. If your dog has itchy skin or digestive issues, talk to your vet about whether a grain-free trial makes sense.
How do I transition my dog to new food?
Always transition gradually over 7-10 days to avoid digestive upset. Start with 25% new food / 75% old food for days 1-3, then 50/50 for days 4-6, then 75/25 for days 7-9, and finally 100% new food on day 10. Watch for loose stool or reduced appetite and slow down the transition if needed.
